\section{Working with small prose fragments}

Collaboration here does not mean ``everyone fights inside the same Word file until the track changes catch fire.'' It means a singer can tighten language while an engraver fixes beams, and neither person has to hold their breath about merge conflicts.

Each chapter section file is encouraged to stay modest in length: one named section, or two sections that truly belong in the same conversation. Humane scale matters. A singer scanning the tree on a small screen should recognize the chapter they need without scrolling through unrelated material, and a proofreader can open only the scene they promised to check.

\textbf{Chord symbol example.} Harmony in words still deserves consistent symbols. You might write ``listen for a \MusicChord{root=D, acc=flat, quality=dom, seven=7} color here'' and let the template handle accidentals and spacing instead of hand-formatting every glyph.

\textbf{Citation example.} Longer references to manuals or articles belong in the shared bibliography file. The LilyPond community maintains approachable engraving documentation, cited here as \cite{lilypondDoc}, so readers who want to dig deeper know exactly where to continue reading.
