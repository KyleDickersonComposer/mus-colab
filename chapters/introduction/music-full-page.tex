\section{Tall music in normal margins}

Longer scores do not have to mean ``export a PDF from another program and paste it like a ransom note.'' You keep the engraving in LilyPond and let LaTeX argue about vertical space on your behalf.

Long excerpts can stay on ordinary text pages while still \emph{sharing vertical space} between systems. You give a target height for the whole figure; the layout engine then stretches the gaps between systems so a short passage floats comfortably in the frame and a tall passage breathes evenly instead of leaving one giant hole under the last staff.

\beginBookExample{Scale Etude}
\beginFixedHeightMusicExample{0.50\textheight}
\lilypondfile{chapters/introduction/excerpt/c-major-etude.ly}
\finishFixedHeightMusicExample
\finishBookExample

The scale étude above shows the pattern: adjust the height until the spread matches your taste, and keep using the same house engraving defaults as other block examples so staff width still matches the type block.

When a passage must continue onto another page, a related pair of helpers keeps the same spacing idea and forces a page break only at the system you name; the source comments next to those macros spell out the two arguments.
